Pagudpud

Province of Ilocos Norte

Pagudpud, prior to its creation as a municipality, was a barrio of the town of Bangui. It has self-propelling and self-governing populace whose elders thought of separating it from its mother town. Pagudpud is known for its beautiful white-sand beaches like Saud Beach, often called the "Boracay of the North," as well as its dramatic natural landscapes such as the Bangui Windmills, Blue Lagoon, and Patapat Viaduct. It offers a serene coastal retreat with turquoise waters and is a center for local culture and traditions, celebrated through events like the Kangayedan Festival.

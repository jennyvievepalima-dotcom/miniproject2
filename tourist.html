Tourist Spots

Pagudpud is a coastal town located at the northernmost tip of Ilocos Norte. It is known as the “Boracay of the North”, but it has so much more to offer apart from its pristine coastline. Pagudpud's Bangui Bay also features 20 wind turbines, which makes it Southeast Asia's first windmill farm.

    • Patapat Viaduct

    The Patapat Viaduct, also known as the Patapat Bridge, is a viaduct in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte, a coastal resort town in the northernmost tip of the island of Luzon in the Philippines.

    The bridge is elevated 31 meters (102 ft) over sea level. It is a concrete coastal bridge 1.3 kilometers (0.81 mi) long, 9.42 meters (30.9 ft) wide,[1] and connects the Maharlika Highway and the MacArthur Highway from the Ilocos Region to Cagayan Valley. The viaduct was constructed by Hanil Development Co. Ltd. under the overall management of DPWH-PMO-PJHK and was completed and opened to traffic in October 1986. It rises along the town's coastal mountains, which is the starting point of the Cordillera Mountain Range that snakes through Northern Luzon. It is the 4th longest bridge in the Philippines.

    • Saud Beach

    Saud Beach is the serene jewel of Pagudpud. Nestled in the northernmost reaches of Luzon, in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte, is often hailed as the “Boracay of the North but that comparison barely scratches the surface. While Boracay dazzles with its nightlife and crowds, Saud Beach offers something far more rare: tranquility, untouched beauty, and a sense of timeless escape.

    The jewel in the crown of Pagudpud is the beach, with its renowned wonderful fine white sand. The waters of the South China Sea are a magnificent blue and crystal clear all the time. This is bordered by spectacular multi-coloured corals and iridescent fish. The beach is lined with over hanging coconut palms, creating a scene from a dream. A popular venue for fashion photo shoots.

    • Paraiso ni Anton

    Paraiso ni Anton (Anton's Paradise) is located just a few meters from the Agua Grande River Park. The cool mountain spring found here is believed to have healing powers, attracting visitors who drink the water or collect it to bring home. Around the area, several stalls sell local products such as fruits, seashell crafts, and colorful pebble souvenirs. Locals also believe the place is enchanted by a White Lady, and as a sign of respect and to avoid disturbing her, motorists passing by the falls traditionally honk their horns three times.

    • Agua Grande

    The Agua Grande River, also called Dakkel a Danum in Ilocano, is known for its eco-friendly and non-carbon-producing mini-hydroelectric power plant. Commissioned in 1983, it has been providing renewable energy to the towns of Pagudpud, Bangui, Dumalneg, Adams, and Burgos. The river, along with the Mabogabog Falls, supplies the water that powers the Agua Grande turbines. The Ilocos Norte Electric Cooperative also conducted a study on the hydro plant and another similar facility. Built during the Marcos administration, the plant has a power capacity of 4.5 megawatts, enough to provide electricity to around 17,000 households in Pagudpud and nearby areas such as Pasuquin and Bacarra.

    • Bantay Abot Cave

    Bantay Abot Cave is a natural rock formation and popular tourist spot located along the shores of Balaoi, Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte. Despite its name, Bantay Abot actually means “mountain with a hole.” The cave-like arch was once a hill that gradually eroded after an earthquake. Facing the South China Sea, it offers a scenic view and is situated just a few kilometers from other attractions like Kabigan Falls and the Patapat Viaduct.

    • Dos Hermanos Islands

    Dos Hermanos Island is located in Barangay Balaoi and is known for its two identical, cone-shaped mountains. According to legend, the islands represent two brothers who were deeply devoted to each other. One day, while fishing, they were caught in a strong typhoon and drowned together. As a symbol of their loyalty, they were transformed into the twin mountains that now form Dos Hermanos Island. Beneath the rocks are caves filled with colorful tropical fish, best seen during low tide. The surrounding waters also contain smaller rocks where gamet, a famous edible seaweed, grows during the rainy season. The area lies north of Bangui and its well-known windmills, with the Cordillera Mountain Range standing inland and the South China Sea stretching to the north and west.

    • Kabigan Falls

    Kabigan Falls, located in Barangay Balaoi, is a stunning 120-meter waterfall with cool, clear waters perfect for swimming. It can be reached through group tours offered by local hotels or by hiring a private tricycle, which costs around ₱700 for a round trip from Saud Beach. From the highway, it takes about a 30-minute walk to reach the falls, and the entrance fee already includes a local guide. The trail leading to the falls offers a scenic view of rice fields and a clear stream, making the journey as enjoyable as the destination itself.

    • Little Batanes

    Caunayan Point, often called the “Little Batanes of the North,” is a beautiful tourist destination in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte. It is known for its dramatic cliffs, green hills, and clear blue waters that create a peaceful and stunning view. The area offers wide, scenic landscapes similar to those of Batanes but with its own unique charm. Its calm and relaxing atmosphere makes it a perfect place to unwind and enjoy nature's beauty.

    One of its main highlights is the clean coastline, where gentle waves touch the shore, inviting visitors to take relaxing walks along the beach. The rock formations and colorful sunsets make it a great spot for photography. For those who love adventure, Caunayan Point also offers hiking trails that lead to hidden coves and quiet beaches. Tourists can also visit nearby villages to experience the warm hospitality and culture of Ilocos. Whether you want to relax, explore, or take in the scenic views, Caunayan Point is a must-visit place that offers a truly unforgettable experience.

    • Stingray Memorial

    The Caunayan Landing Site in Sitio Liwliwa at Bimmanaaw holds great historical importance in both Philippine and World War II history. This quiet and secluded bay was the location of the first successful landing in the northern Philippines during the war. Also known as the Stingray Memorial, it was built to honor the bravery of Filipino bolomen, guerrillas, and the crews of 20 American submarines that supplied them with weapons and materials. Sponsored by the Philippine Veterans Bank, the memorial is made of marble, stone, and pebbles, and is topped with a 500-kilogram anchor. The Caunayan Landing Site stands as a lasting reminder of courage, sacrifice, and unity during a crucial time in history.
